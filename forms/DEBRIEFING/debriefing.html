This debriefing form should give you a short description of what the research experiment you
just conducted is about. If you would like more detail, please email the experimenter (contact
information is above).
In a recognition experiment, participants discriminate words that were studied in a training list
from words that were not presented in the training list. As you may have noticed, in this study
the amount of word relatedness was altered. Specifcally, words that had usual pronunciations and unusual
pronoucniations were selected. We hope to obtain a distinctiveness effect, where unusual words have a
memorial benefit when presented among a list of usual words. In addition, some of these words you were
instructed to type, and others to simply read. We hope to obtain a well known Production Effect,
where produced words are better remembered than unproduced words. In conjunction with this, we
are going to use a computational model of natural language processing, called Latent Semantic
Analysis, to derive predictions that are consistent with human performance. The ultimate goal of
this research is to be able to derive predictions about distinctiveness a priori using computational
models. This study contained no deception or experimental manipulation of any kind. You can learn
more about this line of work from our laboratory webpage located at:
home.cc.umanitoba.ca/~jamiesor.
